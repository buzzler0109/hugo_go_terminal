The public organization SHUM CAMP brings together specialists from the international community who work on analytical and research projects in the fields of open data, documentation of war crimes, and countering disinformation. We cooperate with law enforcement and judicial authorities, as well as international partners, providing evidence-based analysis in the context of the armed conflict launched by russia against Ukraine.

**What You Will Be Doing**

Depending on the focus area, you may work with:

* Searching for and collecting data from open sources;
* Analyzing information to identify facts, connections, and patterns;
* Conducting geospatial intelligence (geolocation of photos and videos, working with maps and satellite imagery);
* Analyzing technical assets, weapons, and military equipment (STI / technical intelligence);
* Verifying photo, video, and text materials;
* Preparing analytical reports.

The specific area of work will be determined based on your skills and interests.

**Who We Are Looking For**

* Individuals ready for independent and responsible work;
* Those who can think critically and work with large volumes of information;
* People who are attentive to detail and context;
* Volunteers willing to dedicate time to regular volunteer activity (flexible schedule).

**Advantages Will Be**

* A strong command of English or other languages for working with international sources;
* Experience in OSINT, GEOINT, analytics, journalism, or related fields;
* Understanding of military equipment, weapons, or technical specifications;
* Experience working with mapping services and satellite imagery;
